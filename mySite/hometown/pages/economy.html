Like any city or town situated nearby a ski resort, Golden sees a significant increase in tourism (and, subsequently, income) in winter and early spring when slopes are at their peak – pun intended. It should come as no surprise then that the labour force is dominated by sales and service and management jobs, most of which have to do with the Kicking Horse Mountain Resort situated a short drive away; however, trades and transport hold a large portion of the work force as well.

Other sectors – though not as significant – are also represented in Golden, so finding work is of little concern for most living here: Education, law, and government jobs make up as much a portion of the work force as management occupations. That said, however, as is expected in a town of this size, occupations in sciences, arts, and sports are available but they are few and far between. In Golden, jobs in these sectors aren’t exactly what one would call plentiful, though they do exist, in large part due to the College of the Rockies campus located in Golden. Golden also boasts an above-average household income of $70,000 per year – though a large portion of families are making $100,000 or more, and many generate well above the $150,000 mark annually.
